Every summer in Rio de Janeiro, just before Lent, the city transforms into an explosion of music, dance, and pure joy. From February 13 to February 21, 2026, you can experience the heart and soul of Brazilian culture wrapped up in one eight-day celebration.

The Rio Carnival has long evolved from its Portuguese colonial roots, blending African and indigenous influences to create the world's largest carnival celebration. According to GetYourGuide, it's one of the best times to visit Rio. From the incredible Sambadrome parade to street parties, here's everything you need to know about Rio Carnival 2026.

**When and where**

The official dates for Rio Carnival 2026 are February 13 to February 21. The main event is the Sambadrome parade, where samba schools compete in a dazzling display of costumes, floats, and choreography. The Sambadrome is located in downtown Rio, near the Praça Onze metro station.

**Sambadrome tickets**

Tickets for the Sambadrome go on sale months in advance. They range from cheap bleacher seats (R$ 50-150) to luxury boxes with open bar. GetYourGuide recommends buying early, as the best seats sell out fast. The parade runs from 9 PM to 5 AM, so pace yourself.

**Street parties (blocos)**

For a more authentic experience, join a bloco. These free street parties happen all over the city, led by a truck with live music. The most famous is Banda de Ipanema, which attracts thousands. Check the official schedule at riocarnival.com.

**What to wear**

Comfort is key. Wear light clothes, comfortable shoes, and a money belt. Costumes are optional but fun. Many locals wear themed T-shirts or sparkly accessories. Avoid wearing anything too expensive.

**Safety tips**

Rio is generally safe during Carnival, but pickpocketing is common. Keep your phone and wallet secure. Stick to well-lit areas and travel in groups. Use official taxis or ride-sharing apps.

**Where to stay**

Book accommodation early, as prices skyrocket. Popular neighborhoods include Copacabana, Ipanema, and Leblon. Hostels and budget hotels fill up fast. Consider staying in Santa Teresa for a bohemian vibe.

**Food and drink**

Street vendors sell caipirinhas, beer, and snacks like coxinha and pastel. Stay hydrated with coconut water. For a proper meal, try a churrascaria (Brazilian barbecue).

**Getting around**

Metro runs 24 hours during Carnival, but stations near the Sambadrome get crowded. Buses and taxis are also available. Walking is best for short distances.

**Final tips**

Arrive early for the Sambadrome parade to avoid lines. Bring cash, as not all vendors accept cards. Pace your energy – Carnival is a marathon, not a sprint. Most importantly, let loose and dance like a carioca.

Rio Carnival 2026 promises to be an unforgettable experience. Whether you're watching the Sambadrome parade or dancing in a bloco, you'll leave with memories that last a lifetime.
